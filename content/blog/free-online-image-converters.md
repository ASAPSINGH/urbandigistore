## 🎨 PNG vs. JPG vs. WebP: Key Differences

Understanding the structural differences between image formats is key to choosing the right extension:

*   **PNG (Portable Network Graphics)**: A **lossless** compression format. It retains 100% of the original image detail and supports transparent backgrounds (alpha channels). This makes PNGs ideal for logo graphics, charts, and text screenshots, but file sizes can be very large.
*   **JPG / JPEG (Joint Photographic Experts Group)**: A **lossy** compression format. It reduces file sizes dramatically by discarding visual information that the human eye is unlikely to notice. JPGs do not support transparency and are best for high-color photographs.
*   **WebP**: The modern next-generation web format developed by Google. It supports both lossy and lossless compression, preserves alpha transparency, and results in file sizes that are **25% to 30% smaller** than equivalent PNGs or JPGs.

## 🔐 The Security Risk of Server-Side Image Converters

Most free image conversion platforms on the web require you to upload your files to their external servers. This presents significant security risks:

1.  **Data Retention**: Many sites store your uploaded images on cloud storage buckets for hours, days, or even permanently.
2.  **Privacy Exposure**: If your images contain sensitive information (like bank statements, ID copies, or proprietary designs), uploading them exposes you to data breaches.
3.  **Server Logs**: Web servers log metadata about your files, including filenames and origin IP addresses.

To prevent this, use a **browser-based image converter** that utilizes client-side Canvas and WebAssembly APIs. Your files are converted entirely in your local system memory and never touch a remote server.
