# How to Draw Bull Market Fibonacci Fans

Understanding technical chart lines is key to planning successful trades. Here is a direct breakdown of how this calculation works.

A **Fibonacci technical indicator** is a charting tool that projects support and resistance zones based on mathematical ratios (e.g., 38.2%, 61.8%). By connecting price extremes, traders identify trend pivot points and plan entry coordinates.

In technical analysis, rising prices do not climb in a straight vertical line. Bull markets are defined by series of higher highs and higher lows, where retracements pull back to find dynamic, diagonal support. To map these diagonal support speed lines during an uptrend, traders use **Fibonacci Fans**.

In this guide, we'll explain how to draw bullish Fibonacci fans, detail the diagonal angle calculations, and map long entry zones.

## 📐 Bullish Fibonacci Fans: The Concept

A bullish Fibonacci fan uses the price-time coordinates of a rising impulse leg to project three diagonal support lines:
*   **The Trendline**: The baseline connecting the low to the high.
*   **The Fan Lines**: Lines extending from the low that cross an invisible vertical line at the Fibonacci ratios (38.2%, 50.0%, and 61.8%).
*   These lines act as dynamic support zones where pullbacks are expected to find buying pressure and resume the uptrend.

## 📏 Step-by-Step Drawing Guide

In your charting software, select the **Fibonacci Fan** tool:

1.  **Click Anchor 1 (The Swing Low)**: Click on the trough where the upward trend leg began.
2.  **Click Anchor 2 (The Swing High)**: Drag up-and-right to click on the peak of the rally before the correction started.

The tool will draw three diagonal rays extending from the Swing Low:
*   **38.2% Fan Line**: The steepest diagonal support. Bounces that hold here indicate an extremely strong, accelerating bull market.
*   **50.0% Fan Line**: The middle speed line. Serves as a standard correction pivot.
*   **61.8% Fan Line**: The ultimate trend frontier. Bounces must hold above this diagonal line; a breakdown below the 61.8% fan line signals that the bullish trend has failed.
