# The Complete Guide to Position Sizing and Risk Management in Trading

No matter how accurate your trading strategy is, you will eventually fail without proper **risk management**. The difference between successful professional traders and amateurs is not the win rate; it is how they manage their risk per trade. 

In this guide, we will break down the exact mathematics of position sizing, the 1% risk rule, and how to place stop losses using technical levels like Fibonacci retracements.

---

## 🛡️ The Golden Rule: Account Risk vs. Trade Risk

Before opening any position, you must understand the difference between **Account Risk** and **Trade Risk (Position Size)**:

*   **Account Risk**: The percentage of your total trading capital you are willing to lose if a trade hits your stop loss. The standard recommendation is **1% to 2%** of your account balance.
*   **Trade Risk (Position Size)**: The actual dollar size or amount of contract/shares you buy or sell.

If you have a $10,000 account and use a **1% risk rule**, your maximum loss on any single trade must not exceed **$100**. This ensures that even if you hit a streak of 10 consecutive losses, you will still retain 90% of your capital.

---

## 📐 How to Calculate Your Position Size

Calculating your exact position size requires three inputs:
1.  **Account Balance**: Your total trading capital (e.g., $10,000).
2.  **Risk Percentage**: The percentage you want to risk (e.g., 1%).
3.  **Stop Loss Distance**: The percentage difference between your entry price and your stop loss price (e.g., 2%).

The formula to calculate your position size is:

$$\text{Position Size} = \frac{\text{Account Balance} \times \text{Risk \%}}{\text{Stop Loss Distance \%}}$$

### Example Calculation:
If you are entering a Bitcoin long trade:
*   **Account Balance**: $5,000
*   **Risk**: 1% ($50 max loss)
*   **Entry Price**: $60,000
*   **Stop Loss**: $58,800 (Stop Loss Distance = 2%)

$$\text{Position Size} = \frac{\$5,000 \times 0.01}{0.02} = \$2,500$$

To execute this trade, you will buy **$2,500** worth of Bitcoin (or 0.0417 BTC). If Bitcoin falls to $58,800, you will lose exactly $50 (1% of your account).

## 🌀 Using Fibonacci Retracement for Stop Loss Placement

One of the most effective ways to find where to place your stop loss is by using **Fibonacci Retracements**. When an asset is in an uptrend, it pullbacks to key Fibonacci levels before continuing upwards:

*   **0.382 Level**: Shallow retracement (indicates a strong trend).
*   **0.500 Level**: Mid-point retracement (psychological support).
*   **0.618 Level (Golden Ratio)**: The most critical support zone.

### Setup Guide:
1.  Draw the Fibonacci retracement from the recent swing low to the swing high.
2.  Set your entry trigger around the **0.618 level**.
3.  Place your stop loss slightly below the **0.786 level** or the swing low to avoid getting stopped out by brief liquidity sweeps.

By placing your stop loss systematically below Fibonacci levels, you align your risk management with natural market mathematical structures.
