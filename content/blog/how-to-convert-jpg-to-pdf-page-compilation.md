# How to Convert JPG to PDF: Page Compilation

Managing digital documents requires strict compliance with formatting and font subsets. Here is a direct definition of this standard.

**PDF (Portable Document Format)** is a digital document standard designed to preserve formatting across all devices. Modern PDF operations utilize local browser APIs to merge, split, or secure pages without server uploads, protecting document data.

When organizing scanned receipts, presentation slides, or photos, merging them into a single file makes them much easier to share. Converting **JPG to PDF** compiles multiple image pages into a single, clean document layout.

## 📐 Key Steps in Image-to-PDF Compilations

To combine your images into a structured PDF document layout:
*   **Page Scaling**: Ensure the compiler dynamically scales the image to fit standard page dimensions (e.g., A4 or Letter) while preserving its original aspect ratio.
*   **Drag-and-Drop Reordering**: Order your pages logically by dragging thumbnail previews before compiling.
*   **Marginal Borders**: Add layout borders (e.g., 0.5 inches) to prevent images from clipping when printed.
